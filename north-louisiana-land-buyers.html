Why North Louisiana Land Owners Are Selling Right Now

North Louisiana has its own distinct land market — driven by timber, agriculture, oil and gas, and the Shreveport-Bossier metro. But the dynamics are shifting, and landowners who act now are capturing value that may not be there in a few years.

Shreveport-Bossier Is Stabilizing

After years of population decline, the Shreveport-Bossier metro is finding new footing through healthcare, aerospace, and logistics employers. This stability is creating renewed developer interest in land within and adjacent to the metro — especially in Bossier and Caddo parishes where new residential and commercial projects are breaking ground.

Timber Land Values Are Cycling

Louisiana's timber industry is a major economic driver, but timberland values have been under pressure as global pulp and lumber markets shift. If you're holding timber acreage as an investment, this cycle may be your opportunity to exit before the next round of consolidation presses prices further down.

Property Taxes on Idle Land Don't Wait

Annual property taxes on undeveloped land in North Louisiana parishes add up whether the land is producing income or not. Many landowners are finding that the carrying cost of idle acreage — especially land that's been in a family for generations — exceeds what they'd make waiting for the next price cycle.

North Louisiana Land Sellers: Here's What We Look At

Most buyers in North Louisiana are looking for timberland or cleared farmland near roads. But Land Kings buys what others overlook:

Swamp and Wetland Parcels

Cypress brakes, wetlands, and low-lying acreage that doesn't perk for residential use. These properties often have timber value, mitigation bank potential, or wildlife management appeal that we factor into offers.

Heirship and Inherited Land

Parcels passed down through generations with multiple heirs, clouded title, or succession issues. We work through Louisiana succession law and close with patience.

Oil and Gas Affected Properties

Land with old well sites, surface casings, or mineral servitude complications. We work with mineral attorneys and environmental consultants to understand the full picture.

Large Timber Tracts

100+ acres of timberland in Caddo, Bossier, Bienville, and surrounding parishes. Institutional timber investors are active in this region — we connect sellers with the right buyers.

Parcels Near I-20 Corridor

Land near the I-20 corridor between Shreveport and Monroe with development potential. Even modest commercial interest along this stretch can unlock value for landowners.

Scrub and Under-Maintained Land

Parcels overgrown with scrub timber, brush, or that need clearing work. We price the restoration costs in and still make competitive offers.
